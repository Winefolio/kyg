1. Antonelli "Trebium" Trebbiano Spoletino 2020
Producer: Antonelli
Year: 2020
Type: White
Region: Spoleto DOC, Umbria, Italy

Description: Antonelli is a historic, family-run estate located in the rolling hills of Umbria, with roots dating back to the 19th century and a deep commitment to organic farming and native varietals. Their “Trebium” is made from Trebbiano Spoletino, a distinct local grape that’s gaining recognition for its complexity and ageability. Grown in the high-altitude vineyards of the Spoleto DOC, the 2020 vintage highlights the potential of this often-overlooked region. It’s a compelling introduction to Italian whites beyond the usual suspects—structured, character-driven, and reflective of Umbria’s quiet winemaking renaissance.

2. Casa D'Ambra Ischia Bianco 2022
Producer: Casa D'Ambra
Year: 2022
Type: White
Region: Ischia DOC, Campania, Italy

Description: Casa d’Ambra is a family-run estate founded in 1888 on the volcanic slopes of Ischia, an island just off Naples in Campania. Today Andrea D’Ambra oversees both their own terraced vineyards and those of local growers, focusing on indigenous varieties like Biancolella and Forastera. The 2022 Ischia Bianco captures the island’s heritage, offering a genuine glimpse into Campania’s lesser-known whites. It’s an inviting introduction to Italian wines beyond the mainstream—rooted in tradition, driven by place, and ideal for discovering the regional character and vinous identity of Ischia DOC.

4. Cascina del Torcc Barolo 2020
Producer: Cascina del Torcc
Year: 2020
Type: Red
Region: Barolo, Piedmont, Italy

Description: Cascina del Torc is a family-owned estate located in the heart of Barolo, Piedmont—an area renowned for its Nebbiolo grape and its centuries-old traditions. The 2020 vintage comes from meticulously tended vineyards on steep, south-facing slopes, where generations of growers have honed the art of cultivating Nebbiolo at its finest. This wine is a superb introduction to Barolo—not for its opulence or power, but for its ability to combine tradition, precision, and a true sense of place, showcasing why this region remains Italy’s benchmark for serious red wine.

5. Val di Suga Rosso di Montalcino 2022
Producer: Val di Suga
Year: 2022
Type: Red
Region: Montalcino, Tuscany, Italy
Description: Val di Suga is a respected estate in Montalcino, known for its unique approach of cultivating vineyards on three distinct slopes of the region—north, south, and east—each contributing to a more complete expression of Sangiovese. While their Brunellos have earned acclaim for decades, their Rosso di Montalcino offers a more approachable way to understand the estate’s philosophy. The 2022 vintage comes from younger vines and is crafted with the same care and precision, making it a great introduction to both the Montalcino region and the potential of Sangiovese when grown in its ideal environment.
